## 1. Knowledge

### 1.1 Definition

**Plato** (428?-348? B.C.) advanced the *<u>classic account of knowledge</u>*

- *Knowledge* : A justified true belief

  1. Believe
  2. Justified (true)
  3. Sufficient envidence

### 1.2 Difference between Knowledge and Belief

We can believe something even if it is false and even if we do not have sufficient evidence for it

### 1.3 Observational/Theoretical Knowledge

- **Observational knowledge** : If a knowledge is about an observational entity, it is an observational knowledge
- **Theoretical knowledge** : If a knowledge is about a theoretical entity, it is a theoretical knowledge

## 2. Three Kinds of Knowledge

### 2.1 Historical Knowledge

How the object developed in its history?

### 2.2 Compositional Knowledge

What it is composed of?

### 2.3 Functional Knowledge

What it does?

### 2.4 Relationship

Three kinds of knowledge reveal different aspects of the same object. They are *<u>not exclusive of one another</u>*, but are instead *<u>complementary to one another</u>*.

- It is not the case that "if we have the historical knowledge about an object, we cannot have the compositional knowledge about it, or vice versa".
- Rather, the three kinds of the knowledge jointly contribute to the complete knowledge about an object.

### 2.5 Application

Suppose that scientists encounter a new material. What should they do to obtain knowledge about it?

They sould investigate

1. Where it came from?
2. What it is composed of?
3. How it behaves?

Once they have done these things, they could answer the question

- What is it?

## 3. Summary

- According to Plato, knowledge is a justified true belief
- We know that dinosaurs went extinct due to a huge meteorite, if and only if

  1. We believe that dinosaurs went extinct due to a huge meteorite
  2. We have enough evidence for the belief
  3. It is true that dinosaurs went extinct due to a huge meteorite
- Scientists investigate the *history*, the *composition*, and the *function* of an object
- As a result, they obtain the *historical, compositional, and functional* knowledge about it

  1. Historical knowledge : how the object developed in its history
  2. Compositional knowledge : what it is composed of
  3. Functional knowledge : What it does
