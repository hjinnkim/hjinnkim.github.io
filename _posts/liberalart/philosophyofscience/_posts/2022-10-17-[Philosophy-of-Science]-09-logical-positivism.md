## 1. What is Logical Positivism

### 1.1 Background

- *<u>Logical positivism</u>* was a movement in philosophy that arose in Berlin and Vienna in the late 1920s
- **Moritz Schlick** (1882-1936), **Rudolf Carnap** (1891-1970), and **Otto Neurath** (1882-1945) were cheif exponents of it
- Logical positivists laid the foundation for *analytic philosophy*
  - The most distinctive feature of analytic philosophy is predcise and rigorous thinking

### 1.2 Features of Logical Postivism

- Logical positivists were deeply distrustful of metaphysical claims
  - Metaphysical claims are the claims about unobservables
  - Such as "Got created the world"
  - You may think that these metaphysical sentences express profound(심오한) truths
- Logical positivits thought that they are meaningless sentences
- **David Hume** (1711-1776) said we can trust mathematics and empirical science (physics, chemistry, biology, and psychology)_, but not metaphysics(이상학)

## 2. The Verifiability Theory of Meaning

### 2.1 Verifiability Theory of Meaning

- The core idea of logical positivism is the *<u>verifiability theory of meaning</u>*
- <u>Verifiability theory of meaning</u> : A sentence is meaningful if and only if it is verifiable by observations
  - i.e., if and only if it can be proved to be true by observations

### 2.2 Objection to the Verifibility Theory of Meaning

- There are counterexamples to it (Hung, 1997)
  - General statements, such as "All crows are black" are meaningful (we know what they mean)
  - But, they are not verifiable by observations
  - Accordingly, meaningfulness is one thing, and verifiability is another
    - i.e., a sentence can be meaningful, although it is not verifiable by observations

### 2.3 Confirmation Theory of Meaning

- In light of the objection, logical positivists might retreat to the *<u>confirmation theory of meaning</u>*
- <u>Confirmation theory of meaning</u> : A sentence is meaningful if and only if it is confirmable (Hung, 1997)
- Confirmability is a weaker notion than verifiability
  - To say that a statement is confirmed means that it is *likely* to be true
- Since previous general statement is confirmable, it is meanigful

### 2.4 Self-Defeatness of Logical Positivism

- Is the verifiability theory of meaning verifiable by obserbations?
  - It is not clear
  - The verifiability theory of meaning invalidates itself (Creath, 2017)
- The ciriticism applies no less to the confirmability theory of meaning
- Therfore, they are self-defeating

## 3. Empirical Science

### 3.1 Empirical Science and Unobservables

- Another objection to logical positivism is that <u>empirical science makes claims about unobservables</u>
  - The kinetic theory of gases holds that a gas is composed of molecules, and molecules move about in accordance with Newton's mechanics
  - Molecules are unobservables
  - So, the verifiability theory of meaning implies that the kinetic theory of gases is meaningless, which is disagreeable

### 3.2 Operationalism

- Logical positivists argue that a scientific theory is meaningful because theoretical theory can be translated into observational sentences
- *<u>Operationalism</u>* by **Percy Williams Bridgman** (1882-1961) : Any concept is meaned by nothing but a set of operations; The concept is synonymous with the corresponding set of operations (Bridgman, 1927)
- The operationalism holds that a theoretical sentence can be translated into a set of observational sentences describing tesing operations and experimental results
- According to operationalism, a theoretical sentence can be translated into a set of conditional sentences whose antecedents(선행사건) describe testing operations and whose consequents describe experimental results
- If you know all the relevant testing operations and experimental results, you know completely what a theoretical sentence means
- Consequently, a theoretical sentence is <u>not a description of unopservables</u>, but rather <u>a set of description of observables</u>
  - Metaphysical sentences are meaningless because they cannot be translated into sets of observational sentences
  - Metaphysical sentences are different in kind from theoretical sentences in science

### 3.3 Objections to Operationalism

Operationalism has received criticisms (Hung, 1997)

1. We cannot specify all the relevant testing operations and experimental result, but we know what a theoretical sentence means
   - Therefore, the meaning of a theoretical sentence cannot be equated with the set of testing operations and experimental results
2. It is difficult to get rid of a theoretical term from a theoretical sentence
3. It is not clear that a theoretical sentence can be perfectly translated into a set of observational sentences
    - Translating the theoretical sentence to observational sentences is to distort the speaker's intention

### 3.4 The Network Theory of Meaning

- *<u>The network theory of meaning</u>* holds that a theoretical term gets its meanings by virtue of occupying certain plces in a network of theoretical beliefs which as a whole meets observations
- It denies that a theoretical term gets its meaning by virtue of being linked to observations individually
- You cannot know what those theoretical expressions mean just by investigating how they are connected with observations

## 4. Psychology

### 4.1 Behaviorism

- Logical positivism is philosophy goes hand in hand with *<u>Behaviorism</u>* in philosophy in the early 20<sup>th</sup> century
- <u>Behaviorism</u> holds that psychology should be concerned with connections between stimuli and responses, but not with the mind
  - The rationale for this view is that stimuli and responses are observable, whereas the mind is not

### 4.2 Objection to Behaviorism

- **Noam Chomsky** (1959) launches a lethal criticism against behaviorism
- His argument, called "the argument from the poverty of the stimulus", states that children can construct sentences that they had never heard before
  - Their verbal behaviors are not determined by their verbal stimuli
  - It follows that a verbal output cannot be explained solely in terms of a verbal input, and that the explanation of verbal behavior should invoke the mind
- This insight opens the door to the advent of a new doctrine in psychology called "cognitivism"
  - Under the framework of cognitivism, psychologists test hypotheses about the mind
    - They hypothesize that the mind has a cognitive system called "ToM" (Theory of Mind)
    - ToM enables us to infer what mental states that other peopleare in
    - If ToM malfunctions, we cannot read other people's minds, and we treat other people as if they are inanimate objects(autism)

## 5. Summary

- Logical positivisits trust mathematics and empirical science, but not metaphysics
- According to verifiability theory of meaning, a sentence is meaningful if and only if it is verifiable by observations
- A famous objection to the verifiability theory of meaning is that it is self-defeating
- Operationalism states that a theoretical sentence can be translated into a set of conditional sentences whosse antecedents express a set of testing operations and whose consequents express a set of experimental results
- Two objections to operationalism
  - We can kmow what a theoretical sentence means, although we do not know all about the testing operations and experimental results
  - We cannot remove a theoretical term from a theoretical sentence by translating it into a set of conditional sentences
- Behaviorism claims that psychology should not set up hypotheses about the mind, and that it should rather investigate the connections between stimuli and responses
  - Behaviorism is predicated on the view that stimuli and responses are observable, whereas mental states are not
- Behaviorism cannot account for verbal behaviors
  - As a result, it is replaced by cognitivism
