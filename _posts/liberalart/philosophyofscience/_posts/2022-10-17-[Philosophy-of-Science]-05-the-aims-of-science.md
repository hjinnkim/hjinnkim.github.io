## 1. What is Science

### 1.1 Science as an enterprise

- Science is an enterprise just like music, sports, religion, and politics
- In other words, science is a collections of acitivities, just as, say, music is a collection of activities
- However, unlike musicians, scientists set up and test hypotheses
- A hypothesis is an assumption about the world and to test it is to determine whether it is true or false
- Scientists observe the world or perform experiments to determine whether it agrees or disagrees with the world

### 1.2 Aim of Science

- Science is a set of acitivities of setting up and testing hyotheses
- What does science pursue?
- The aims of science should be distinguished from the aims of scientists
- Scientists may have the aims of publishing papers in prestigious journals, building their reputations, securing their research funds, and supporting their families
- These aims are not the aims of science

## 2. True Explanations, True Predictions, and Useful Manipulations

### 2.1 Prof. Park's thought

- Science is a set of acitivities of setting up and testing hypotheses
- According to Prof. Park, they aim for *true explanations*, *true predictions*, and *useful manipulations*.

### 2.2 True Explanations

- Science pursues true explanations
  1. The pollen on the surface of water moves around
      - According to the kinetic theory, water molecules are in constant motion, and they bombard the pollen
  2. A planet moves around the sun
      - According to Newton's the first law of motion, a planet has the tendency to move in a straight line
      - According to Newton's law of gravity, the sun exerts the gravitation force on it
  3. A burning object loses its weight because it loses the invisible substance called "phlogiston"
  4. A hot object becomes cold becuase it loses the invisible substance called "caloric"
- True explantations give rise to the understanding of phenomena ($i, ii$), while false explanations give rise to the misunderstanding of phenomena ($iii, iv$)

### 2.3 True Predictions

- Science pursues true predictions (McMullin, 1993)
  1. Astronomers observe space and then predict when an asteroid will hit the earth
  2. Molecular biologists investigate our genes and then predict what diseases we are likely to get and when we are likely to die
- If predictions are true, predicted events will occur
- If predictions are false, predicted events will not occur

### 2.4 Useful Manipulations

- Science pursues useful manipulations
  1. Molecular biologists manipulate genes
      - As a result, they create new crops and cure us of diseases
  2. Medical scientists extract venoms from snakes
      - Thereby developing antivenoms
  3. Physicists manipulate plutonium
      - Thereby making atomic bombs
- Some manipulations are useful, while other manipulations are useless
  - The manipulations of plutonium was perhas useless

## 3. Scientific Realists and Antirealists

### 3.1 Observational Statements vs. Theoretical Statements

- Observational statements are descriptions of observables
- Theoretical statements are descriptions of unobservables
- Both scientific realists and antirealists agree with we have sufficient evidence for observational statements
- They disagree on whether we have have sufficient evidence for theoretical statements

### 3.2 Different Views about What Science Pursues

- Realists say that science aims for observational and theoretical truths
- Antirealists say that science aims for observational truths but not for theoretical truths

### 3.3 Niels Bohr vs. Albert Einstein

- Niels Bohr (1885-1962) said, "It is wrong to think that the task of physics is to find out how Nature is. Physics concerns what we can say about Nature" (Seargent, 2015)
- Albert Einstein (1879-1955) said "Physics is an attempt to conceptually to grasp reality as it is thought independently of its being observed. In this sense, one speaks of physical reality" (Kiley, 1969)
- To put roughly, Bohr claims that phycis aims to give us observational truths, whereas Einstein claims that phycis aims to give us not only observational truths but also theoretical truths
- In other words, Bohr claims that physics only aims to make true predictions about what we can observe, whereas Einstein claims that physics also aims to correctly describe what we cannot observe

### 3.4 Difference Views about Explanations

- Realists claim that science aims to determine whether the theoretical statement is true or false and whether the explanation is true of false
- Antirealists claim that science has achieved what it needs to achieve when the explanation is advanced, and it does not matter whether the explanation is true or false

### 3.5 Difference Views about Predictions

- Realists claim, while antirealists do not, that science aims to determine whether the theoretical statement is true or false, and whether the prediction is true or false
- But about observational predictions, both realists and antirealists agree that science aims to determine whether the predictions is true or false

### 3.6 Different Vies about Manipulations

- Realists claim that manipulating gems, producing new crops, and curing us of diseases are aims of science
- Antirealists claim that manipulating genes is not an aim of science but producing new crops and curing us of diseasdes are aims of science

## 4. Skepticism about Whether Science Has an Aim

### 4.1 Does Science Have an Aim?

- In prof. Park's view, it is debatable whether science has an aim (Park, 2022)
- Aristotle thought that the world is similar to an organism, whereas modern scientists thought that the world is similar to a machine
    -  An organism has an aim, whereas a machine does not
- The different worldviews resulted in different views about what counts as legitimate explantaions in science
    - Aristotle thought that both *mechanical* and *teleological* explanations are legitimate in science
    - Modern scientists thought that only *mechanical* explanations are legitimate in science

### 4.2 Mechanical vs. Teleological Explanations

- In a mechanical explanation, an event is explained in terms of a cause
- In a teleological explanation, an event is explained in terms of its aim
  
  1. Mechanical Explanations
      - It rains because the air pressure is low
  2. Teleological Explanations
      - It rains so that trees and grasses can grow
- Teleological explanations are rejected in current science
- Only mechanical explanations are accepted in current science

### 4.3 Issue of Whether Science Has an Aim

- It is a teleological view that science has an aim
- Philosophers of science should not believe that science hasd an aim any more than scientists believe that the world has an aim
- On the other hand, science is different from the world in that science is a human enterprise
- Human beings have consciousness, and it sounds legitimate to say that human beings have aims or goals
- Therefore, it also sounds legitimate to say that science has aims or goals

## 5. Summary

- Science is the enterprise of setting up and testing hypotheses
- The aims of such acitivities are true explanations, true predictions, and useful manipulations
- Scientific realists and antirealists disagree on whether science aims to give us theoreticakl truths
- In a mechanical explanation, an event is explained in terms of a cause
- In a teleological explanation, an event is explained in terms of its aim, goal, end, or purpose
- It is a teleological view that science has aims
- Accordingly, it is controversioal whether science has an aim
